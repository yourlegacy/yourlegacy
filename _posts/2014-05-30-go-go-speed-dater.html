Why did I try FastLife’s Speed dating services? Because I don’t want to die alone. When I go out to bars in an attempt to meet single people, my batting average is low, not like Barry Bonds pre-steroids low, but low like the testosterone levels of a 39 year old man. It’s also getting harder to meet people in Toronto, so I decided to get creative.

Before trying FastLife, I forayed into the online dating world by creating an OkCupid profile. Oddly enough, my rate of success was lower than a Trinidadian limbo dancing champion. I couldn’t even tell you why my success rate sucked; I got zero dates. I’m not even sure how that’s possible because I’m awesome.

Everything is awesome, including me.

I have an outgoing, extroverted personality and I write for a living. This should be a recipe for success, at least when it comes to messaging strangers online. After being active online for two months and sending out more messages than the Canada Post office (well over 600), I was dateless.

It’s not like I’m ugly or a troll or anything; I’m often described as cute. My friend calls me Bradley Mini-Cooper because of my hair style and height. My personality has been referred to as charming and asshole-ish on different days by different people. Also, I tend to see eye to eye with women, as I’m relatively short. But I do have a few inches elsewhere...

Not an ugly guy.

My friends, both male and female, helped me craft my OkCupid profile. They even helped me with which pictures to use. Fun fact, don’t post a picture with your shirt off. Luckily for me, my chest hair negates that potential issue. After realizing how much time it was taking to read each profile (read: skim) and sending out personalized messages, I thought Speed Dating would be a better and more efficient approach. Not what it was like.

She suggested I place my coat at coat check, which was a great idea since I did about 30 pushups before entering the venue and the jacket would only obstruct my pumping pectorals.

As people came in, I felt like I was in the NBA draft combine. Men were gazing at women, the women were checking out men. Everyone was analyzing each others appearance, attire, their vertical & bench pressing ability. It was like a hyper version of what most single people feel like at a bar. I couldn’t help but smile; this felt like home and I was excited. The event was was called Successful Singles Speed Dating + Open Bar and was for men between the ages of 24-32, while the women ranged from 21-29. I'm a baby-faced 27 year old, but with a beard I actually look my age. And since the word successful is relative, I felt comfortable. I briefly spoke with “Mandrew” and “Mashley” before the hostess rang the bell and explained the details.

Everyone got a card with your name on it. During each date, you write down the name of the person you are with, and if you connect with them you mark off “yes.” Both parties have to say “yes” for you to receive each other’s email address the next day. The dates were 6 minutes long due to the time constraints of hosting 17 dates in total. Most importantly, the hostess stated not to talk about work. Seems easy enough. The bell rang and the dates began!

I had 17 dates with different people, which is probably more than most people have in a year. Almost all the dates were exciting; most people were interesting and the fact that you only had 6 minutes to conversate almost negated any possibility of having a boring date. I had only one date that was completely disinterested, which, statistically speaking, was bound to happen.

As an outgoing and funny guy, I was a little surprised. I wanted to get on the table and quote Maximus Aurelius.

“Are you not entertained? Is this not why you are here?”

Speed Dating Dos and Don'ts

Your Legacy Inc. spoke with Neha Wadha, FastLife’s event planner and expert on speed dating. Check out her Do’s and Don’ts to ensure your speed dates go smoother than a baby’s behind. Get the dates you want with her expert advice.

The Do’s

  1. Just Relax
    Dating can be extremely nerve-racking, but FastLife’s goal is for everyone to have a fun night meeting like-minded singles in their city. Remember, everyone is in the same boat as you (call it The Love Boat), so just relax and be yourself! You only get 8 minutes and it flies by!

  2. Ask Questions & Listen To The Answers
    Ask “what do you like to do for fun” rather than “what do you do for work.” This opens up the conversation to a wide range of things you may find out you have in common instead of exchanging each other's work routine.

  3. Dress To Impress
    Wear something that you feel the most confident in. Treat the event like it’s a first date because it is. Have some clean clothes and matching socks.

  4. Say “Yes”
    You are meeting a series of different personalities so let your guard down. Let’s be real, saying “yes” to someone doesn't mean you’re marrying them. You never know who you can meet through them and you might even make a friend.

  5. Good Hygiene
    You’re sitting close to someone in an intimate setting, so pack some mints or gum to ensure you have fresh breath.

  6. Meet Everyone & Be Open Minded
    This is the best mindset to have before going to your Speed Date. If you come across someone who you’re not interested in, don’t skip them. You’re at a dating event -- meet everyone with an open mind.

The Don'ts

  1. Don’t Have Verbal Diarrhea (don’t only talk about yourself)
    Or have verbal constipation. Never talk about your ex-girlfriends/boyfriends on your speed date. You only have 8 minutes and no one wants to hear about your past relationships! Make your date feel like a star since you’re looking for compatibility. Give them full eye contact (eye contact, not thigh) and show interest by asking questions.

  2. Don’t Get Drunk
    Speed Dating is nerve-racking and a drink or two is always great to loosen up. Don’t take this too far as this is your first impression with a series of people whom you’ve never met before!

  3. Don’t Ask Someone Their Age
    This is a huge no no on the first date, never ask a woman her age no matter what! There is plenty of time to figure this out in the future.

  4. Don’t Be A Drill Sergeant!
    This isn’t an interrogation. It’s great to ask questions but wait to hear the answer before you move onto the next one. The goal is to have a conversation, not an interview!

  5. Avoid Having The Same Conversation on each date
    Talk about something different on each date. Be creative.

  6. Avoid These Heavy Topics
    Don’t talk about religion (unless it’s a specified religious event), politics, past dates, pick-up lines, news or sex. Or the weather. Never talk about the weather.

  7. DON’T TEXT

I decided not to as I didn’t want my evening to end like Maximus’ did. Throughout the evening, I was asked several times what I did for a living - a cardinal sin according to the hostess - by several dates. I really wanted to say “if I told you, I’d have to kill you” but thought that answer wasn’t in my best interest. I opted for questions like: what’s your favourite movie, what’s your favorite color, do you think that most of the men wearing blazers today are doing so to hide their underarm perspiration?

a) Single People
